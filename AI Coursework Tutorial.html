Strengths and Weaknesses:¶

Before we examine the practical aspects, let us briefly discuss the advantages and disadvantages of KNN and neural networks. This preliminary understanding will assist you in making informed decisions when selecting the appropriate tool for your machine-learning projects.

K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN):Source[1]

  • Strengths: KNN is well-known for its simplicity and ease of implementation. It is a non-parametric algorithm capable of handling complex decision boundaries.

  • Weaknesses: KNN can be sensitive to outliers and may not perform optimally when dealing with large amounts of data.

Neural Networks:Source[2]

  • Strengths: Neural networks retain the ability to capture complex patterns within data. They are capable of handling large amounts of data and can learn classified features.

  • Weaknesses: Training neural networks can be computationally demanding, and they may require a substantial amount of data to prevent overfitting.
